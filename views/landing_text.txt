<p>
Hydrologic Micro Services, HMS, is a hydrologic and water quality modeling system comprised of data provisioning and modeling components and workflows to facilitate environmental modeling.
The components are available as <a href="/hms/api_doc/">web services</a>, <a href="https://github.com/quanted/hms" target="_blank">desktop libraries</a>, and through a <a href="/hms/">web-based graphical user interface</a>.
The target users for HMS are hydrological and water quality modelers and analysts.
HMS users can use the system components and workflows directly or build their own workflows by linking the components.

</p>
<h2>Structure of HMS web app</h2>
<h3>Components</h3>
<p>An HMS component is a module that encapsulates a set of related functions and data representing a physical process in hydrology or fate and transport of a contaminant in water.  HMS components are presented as web services, software libraries, and web forms.</p>
<h4>Work Flows</h4>
<p>An HMS workflow is an orchestrated pattern of executing components to simulate movement and storage of water and the consequential effects on fate and transport of contaminants in water.</p>
